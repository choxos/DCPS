About DCPS

Dental Caries Population Studies in Canada: A systematic review and analysis project

Project Overview

The Dental Caries Population Studies (DCPS) project represents a comprehensive systematic review and analysis of dental caries prevalence in Canada spanning from 1990 to 2025, with advanced Bayesian modeling projections extending to 2050.

Dental caries remains one of the most prevalent non-communicable diseases globally, affecting individuals across the lifespan and imposing substantial health and economic burdens on healthcare systems. In Canada, despite advancements in dental care and public health interventions, dental caries continues to be a significant public health challenge with disparities across geographic regions, socioeconomic groups, and demographic strata.

Our systematic approach utilizes the DMFT/dmft index (Decayed, Missing, Filled Teeth for permanent and deciduous teeth respectively) as the primary epidemiological indicator for measuring dental caries burden across Canadian populations.

Primary Objectives
  • Estimate national and subnational trends (1990-2025)
  • Project dental caries patterns to 2050
  • Quantify age, sex, and geographic variation
Secondary Objectives
  • Examine DMFT/dmft component disparities
  • Assess socioeconomic and healthcare factors
  • Identify high-burden regions and populations
  • Evaluate water fluoridation impact

Methodology

Our analysis employs a comprehensive Bayesian hierarchical modeling approach using the Integrated Nested Laplace Approximation (INLA) methodology. This advanced statistical framework provides several key advantages:

Hierarchical Structure

Models nested data from individuals within age groups, within geographic regions, within time periods.

Spatial Modeling

Besag-York-Molli√© (BYM2) models capture geographic patterns and spatial autocorrelation.

Temporal Analysis

Random walk priors model smooth temporal trends while capturing changes over time.

Small Area Estimation

"Borrows strength" across spatial and temporal domains for robust estimates in sparse data regions.

Technical Implementation: Our models use negative binomial likelihood to handle overdispersion typical in dental caries count data, with penalized complexity priors to prevent overfitting.

Impact and Applications

The DCPS project generates evidence to support policy decisions and intervention strategies at multiple levels:

Policy Makers

Evidence-based resource allocation and targeted intervention planning

Healthcare Providers

Population-level trends to inform clinical practice and prevention strategies

Researchers

Comprehensive data for hypothesis generation and comparative effectiveness research
